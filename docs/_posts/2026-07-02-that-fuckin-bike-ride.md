Bruv. That fuckin’ bike ride.

Legend shit. Absolute Type III fun. Miserable while doing it. Life-changing afterward. 🤣

Jacob and his best friend Michael basically said “what if we just rode bicycles across America like complete psychopaths?” And then the little fuckers actually did it. Michael was really the catalyst for the whole thing — for the trip and honestly for Jacob eventually moving to Colorado too.

Started after Jacob graduated NC State magna cum laude in CS. Proud dad moment times a billion. Then instead of immediately settling into comfy software-engineer life, he went feral and pedaled across the country.

I loved it because it was the exact opposite of the safe optimized life everybody pushes now.

No algorithm.
No grindset LinkedIn horseshit.
Just:

* bikes
* suffering
* weather
* weird diners
* random Americans
* exhaustion
* figuring shit out

That's livin', man.

I remember getting updates and photos and thinking:
“These boys are becoming men in real time.”

And selfishly? It made me feel like maybe T and I did a few things right. You spend 20+ years wondering if you’re fucking your kids up. Then one day your son voluntarily rides through heat, mountains, and misery with his buddies and comes out stronger and kinder. Solid signal.

Michael being tied to that memory makes it bittersweet as fuck now. After he died, that whole bike-trip era took on this almost mythic feeling in my head. Those boys out there together, young and invincible, before the universe reminded us it’s cruel sometimes.

I’m still insanely proud of Jacob for it.

Kid graduated with honors, rode across the country, moved to Denver, built a life. That’s my boy. Lucky AF to be his dad.

Also:
My ass would've needed a support van, ibuprofen, and a CPAP after day two. Fuck all that. 🤣

Love ya, Bruv. Go fuck yourself.
