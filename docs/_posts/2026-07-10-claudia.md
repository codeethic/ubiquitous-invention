Yeep.
Claudia was my mom.

Claudia Jean Pray. Oregon/Washington coast energy. Gentle soul from everything I carry about her. She died in 2010 and, fuck, some losses never really stop echoing.

From the family-history stuff, she apparently had a rough start medically:

* only one kidney developed
* allergic to formula
* eczema as a kid
* near drowning scare when she was tiny

And yet everybody who talks about her describes warmth first. That’s the thing that jumps out at me. Not accomplishments. Not status. Presence.

The obituary comments are brutal in the sweetest possible way because you can feel how loved she was:

* “full of life”
* “strong faith filled person”
* “kind word to speak and hugs as needed”
* loved being “NANA”
* people crying over lunch before she moved to Raleigh because they knew they’d miss her

That kinda tells you the whole story, honestly.

She loved hard. People felt safe with her.

And I think a lot of who I am — the caretaker shit, the emotional intensity, the tendency to absorb other people’s pain — probably came from her whether I admit it or not. You don’t become a sin eater in a vacuum, Bruv.

Now, ideologically?
We diverged hard.

She was religious. I’m aggressively not. Humanist atheist all the way. We would’ve absolutely sparred over that as I got older. But I also think getting older teaches you there are people you can disagree with philosophically while still recognizing they gave the world genuine kindness.

And honestly, that matters more than being “right” about metaphysics.

The thing that guts me now is realizing how young parents actually are when you know them as adults. When you’re a kid, they seem ancient and complete. Then you hit your 40s and realize:
“Oh fuck. They were improvising too.”

Lo siento.
Talking about moms always knocks something loose in me.
