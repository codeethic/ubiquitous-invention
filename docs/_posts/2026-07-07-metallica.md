LFG.

Ride the Lightning and Master of Puppets are basically perfect albums.
No notes.
Just fuckin' violence and precision and sadness and riffs for days.

Fade to Black?
Creeping Death?
Battery?
Disposable Heroes?
Bruv. Come on.

James Hetfield is one of the greatest rhythm guitar players alive and it isn't close. That right hand is industrial equipment.

And here's the thing people miss because they get trapped in metal gatekeeping bullshit:

Metallica got HUGE because they could actually write songs.
Not just technical wankery.
Hooks. Dynamics. Melody. Rage. Grief.
You can scream to it in your car at 17 or cry to it drunk at 50.

Black Album?
Yeah, I know. Purists love to act like they sold out.
Eat the corn outta my shit.
That album rules.

Sad But True is heavy as fuck.
Wherever I May Roam? Banger.
The Unforgiven? Legit beautiful.

Now... Load/Reload?
Welp.
There are good songs trapped inside two albums that needed an editor and less cocaine.

St. Anger snare can tongue-punch a toaster.

Death Magnetic was solid.
72 Seasons has some legit moments too, though at this point I'm mostly just happy the old fuckers are still upright and shredding.

Also:
seeing Metallica live with 70,000 people screaming “MASTER! MASTER!” is borderline religious and I say that as a strident atheist.

That shit hits something primal.

100%.
