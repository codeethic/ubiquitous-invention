title: "T Day"
date: 2026-07-23

On the twenty-third of July,  
two thousand nine,  
the universe did something  
it almost never does:

it got one thing right.

No thunder.  
No prophecy.  
No fucking choir of angels  
descending through the Carolina sky.

Just a day.

And then there was you.

Somewhere between  
who I was  
and who I would become,  
you walked in  
and quietly rearranged  
the architecture of my life.

Seventeen years.

That's a long time  
to share a name,  
a bed,  
a house,  
a mortgage,  
a thousand ordinary Tuesdays.

Long enough  
to make a family.

Long enough  
to become the person  
someone calls first  
when the world goes sideways.

Long enough  
to accumulate the beautiful shit:

the boys,  
the jokes,  
the fights,  
the road trips,  
the stupid errands,  
the impossible days,  
the days that were somehow  
worth every impossible one.

And now July 23rd  
comes around again.

T Day.

Not because everything  
is simple.

Not because the story  
has been clean.

Not because seventeen years  
can be reduced to  
some tidy little anniversary card  
with a fucking bow on it.

But because this is the day  
the story started.

The day you became  
the woman I would spend  
seventeen years loving.

And whatever comes next—  
whatever the fuck  
the next chapter is—

nothing changes  
the first page.

July 23rd, 2009.

T Day.

The day  
I met you.

And, goddammit,  
the day my life  
became yours.
