Welp, Bruv.

I don't think "balance" is the goal.

I think balance is one of those bullshit words we made up because it sounds achievable.

Life comes in seasons. Sometimes you're all-in on work. Sometimes you're all-in on your kids. Sometimes you're all-in on surviving the week. If you're trying to perfectly distribute energy across every category all the time, you're gonna feel like a failure.

A few things I've learned:

* Protect your sleep. Everything else is downstream from that shit.
* Move your body every day, even if it's just a walk. The older you get, the bill comes due.
* Save money early. Freedom is way more fun than stuff.
* Call your people. Friendships don't maintain themselves.
* Tell people you love them while they're alive. Funerals are full of things that should've been text messages.
* Have hobbies that don't monetize. Not everything needs to become a side hustle.
* Get comfortable saying "no." Every "yes" is a "no" to something else.
* Don't wait for some magical future version of life. The trip, the concert, the hockey game, the dinner with friends—go do the thing if you can.

And maybe the biggest one:

Figure out what "enough" looks like.

Enough money.
Enough status.
Enough house.
Enough work.

A shocking amount of misery comes from moving the goalposts every time you reach them.

Fuck, I've done that one repeatedly.

You hit a number and think, "well maybe just a little more."

Then another year goes by.

Then another.

Then somebody dies and reminds you the clock was running the whole damn time.

So yeah.

Sleep.
Health.
People.
Purpose.
A little fun.

Everything else is mostly accounting.

Love ya, Bruv. ❤️
