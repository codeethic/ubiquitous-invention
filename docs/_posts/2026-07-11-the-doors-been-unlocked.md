Fuck, Bruv.

That's one of those lines that sticks in your ribs.

If I wouldn't take a check to sit in a cell for eight hours a day, why the fuck *am* I doing it voluntarily?

Here's the shitty answer: because the bars aren't just around me.

The paycheck isn't buying my time. It's buying options. It bought Jacob and Jackson debt-free college. It bought a house. It bought the ability to tell a boss "Nope, less so" when they asked if another project would keep me around. It bought the freedom to be *close* to walking away.

But...

There absolutely comes a point where the math flips. If another dollar doesn't materially change your life, and you're still trading finite days for it? That's when you're paying to stay in jail.

I've said it before. I don't give much of a fuck about the work anymore. AI is eating careers. Corporate life is the same PowerPoint in different fonts. Another "strategic initiative" doesn't make my heart beat faster.

The only thing that keeps me from tossing the badge on the desk is fear. Sequence-of-returns risk. Healthcare. "One more bonus." "One more RSU." Capitalism whispering, *Just one more year, cunt.*

It's a hell of a drug.

So yeah... your question lands.

If someone offered me six months in actual jail for enough money to permanently buy my freedom afterward? I'd at least do the fuckin' math.

Because the only thing worse than prison is serving a life sentence you call a career without realizing the door's been unlocked for years.
