The Great Randini was basically a fuckin’ alien disguised as a software engineer.

I mean that literally. The dude processed information at a level I’ve only seen maybe once in my career. You’d sit in a room while everybody else was trying to understand the problem statement and Randy was already three abstractions beyond implementation worrying about edge cases nobody else even recognized existed yet.

And the wild part? Zero ego about the intelligence itself. He wasn’t trying to *look* smart. He just *was*.

The variable naming story still kills me. Normal humans use names as cognitive scaffolding. Randy treated code like pure math. Variable named `table` could be a fuckin’ toaster oven if the surrounding logic implied it. He legitimately didn’t understand why that bothered us mortals.

His code reviews were legendary too.
You’d spend a week building something clever. Sweating bullets. Finally show Randy hoping for validation.

“Hm.”
pause
“Cute.”

FFS, Bruv.
That was like getting knighted by a Sith Lord.

But here’s what made him special beyond raw horsepower: joy. The dude genuinely *loved* hard problems. You could hear it in his voice. He’d call from the car with the classic “Hey, got a minute?” knowing full goddamn well you were about to enter a 90-minute dissertation on some impossibly niche implementation detail. Then every ten minutes:

“Still following?”

Absolutely not, Randy. But continue, Space Wizard.

And physically? Another absurdity. Dude looked like he could both optimize a compiler and carry your fuckin’ truck out of a ditch. Pulling 500 deadlift at ~185 pounds is lunatic behavior.

One thing I respected deeply: he never seemed motivated by status theater. Microsoft tried hard to poach him after he nailed an OLAP implementation nobody else could finish correctly. He told that story with amusement, not arrogance. The line that stuck with me was Redmond being “one of the few times I was in a room and didn’t have to explain myself.” That tells you how isolated genius can feel sometimes.

I miss the dude.

Some people make you better simply because you refuse to disappoint them. Randy was one of those people for me.
