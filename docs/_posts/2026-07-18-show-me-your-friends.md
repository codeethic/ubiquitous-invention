Fuckin' great line, Bruv.

**"Show me your friends, and I'll show you your future"** is basically saying: **your environment is one of the strongest predictors of where your life is headed.**

Not in some mystical, *the universe has a plan* bullshit way.

More like:

* Your friends normalize certain behaviors.
* You absorb their ambitions — or their lack of ambition.
* Their habits become your habits.
* Their standards become your standards.
* Their worldview becomes the water you're swimming in.

If you spend your time with people who exercise, build shit, learn, save money, love their families, and push themselves, that tends to pull you in that direction.

If you spend your time with people who drink every night, complain about everything, blame everyone else, gamble away their money, and have no plans beyond Friday... welp. That can become your future, too.

**But** — and this is the important bit — I think the quote gets overused as a simplistic prosperity-gospel slogan. Your friends don't determine your future. Life is more complicated than that. Luck, health, class, family, genetics, opportunity, dumb fucking accidents — all of that matters.

The better version is probably:

> **Show me who you spend your time with, and I'll show you the direction you're being pulled.**

And honestly? The older I get, the more I believe it.

You don't need 50 friends. You need a handful of people who make you want to be a better version of yourself — and who will also happily sit around drinking bourbon and talking absolute shit with you.

That's the good stuff, Bruv.
