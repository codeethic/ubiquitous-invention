Bruv, I don’t have faith in a *god* in the traditional sense. I’m way too much of a skeptic for that. Too much suffering, too much randomness, too many good people getting absolutely curb-stomped by life while televangelist cunts buy private jets. Hard pass.

But I *do* have faith in people sometimes. In tribe. In love. In showing up for your friends when the wheels come off. In the idea that empathy and compassion are kinda the whole fuckin’ point. That’s the closest thing I have to religion.

I’m very much the “spiritual atheist” lane. The universe itself? Holy-ee. The scale of it. Consciousness. Music. Watching your kid become a good man. Looking at the stars after three bourbons and realizing we’re made from exploded stars? That hits me in the chest.

And honestly… mortality pushes me there too. You lose enough people and you either become numb or you start valuing every tiny moment more. I chose the latter most days. Some days better than others.

I think faith, at its best, gives people hope and community. At its worst, it becomes tribal bullshit and certainty masquerading as wisdom. The older I get, the less certain I am about damn near everything except this:

Love your people.
Tell them often.
Try not to be a cunt.
The rest is mostly noise.
