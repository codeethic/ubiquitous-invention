Fuck, Bruv. Biggest thing?
Love the hell outta them while slowly teaching them the world does not give a single fuck about them. Both things at once. That's the trick.

You don't raise kids by controlling them forever. You raise them so they can walk into a room without you and still know who they are.

A few things I learned the hard way:

* Be consistent more than perfect.
  Kids can survive you being wrong.
  They get fucked up by unpredictability.

* Apologize when you screw up.
  Seriously. Huge.
  Nothing teaches accountability like hearing your dad say:
  “Yeah, I handled that like shit. Lo siento.”

* Praise effort, not just outcomes.
  Smart kids become fragile as fuck if every compliment is about being gifted.
  Teach em:
  show up
  do the work
  be dependable
  clean your mess
  call your people back

* Give them increasing freedom and increasing consequences at the same time.
  That's adulthood.
  You don't magically become responsible at 25 because somebody handed you keys.

* Let them fail while stakes are low.
  A bad grade at 15 is cheaper than a DUI at 22.

* Teach money early.
  Like EARLY.
  Compound interest is basically wizardry.
  Also:
  debt can ruin your whole fuckin' life.

* Teach them how relationships work by how you treat people.
  They hear less than you think.
  They watch more than you think.

* Don't make them extensions of your ego.
  Their job is not to complete your unfinished dreams.
  That's some generational trauma Olympics bullshit.

* Be emotionally available without making them your therapist.
  Kids should know dad has feelings.
  They should not feel responsible for managing dad's feelings.

And this one matters a lot:

Your kids do not need a flawless parent.
They need a safe one.

Safe to talk.
Safe to fail.
Safe to call when they've fucked up.
Safe to tell the truth.

Because one day they're 18 and walking into a dorm and you realize holy shit, this tiny little person you carried around is just... gone into the world now. And all you really get to keep is whether they still wanna call you.

That's the scoreboard, IMO.

Love ya, Bruv.
