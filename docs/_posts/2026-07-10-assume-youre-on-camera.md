Fuck, Boys.

Assume you're on camera.

Always.

Not because anybody gives a shit about your breakfast burrito. Because every dumb decision, every drunk joke, every fight, every "this'll be hilarious" moment can end up preserved forever by some asshole holding a phone.

When I was young, you could do something stupid on Friday night and it died by Monday.

Y'all don't get that luxury.

A few things I'd tell Jacob and Jackson:

* Don't put anything in writing you wouldn't want read aloud at a job interview.
* Don't send pictures you wouldn't want on a billboard.
* Don't let your friends record you when alcohol, drugs, or stupidity are involved.
* If someone says "bro, this won't leave this group chat," assume it already has.
* The internet has a longer memory than people do.

But.

Don't become paranoid either.

The answer isn't hiding from life.

Go on the trip.
Kiss the girl.
Dance like an idiot.
Sing badly.
Do dumb *harmless* shit with your friends.

Just don't be the fool doing felony-level stupidity while six phones are pointed at him.

The thing I'd worry about most isn't embarrassment. It's context. A 10-second clip can make the best person on earth look like a proper cunt.

So give people less ammunition.

And one more thing, Bruv.

Your character matters more than your image.

If you're a good man, work hard, treat people right, and own your mistakes, a bad photo or awkward video usually blows over.

If you're an asshole, the cameras eventually catch up to you.

They always do.

Work hard. Play hard.

Just remember somebody's probably recording the "play hard" part. 😜
