Welp.

I'm Josh. Fifty-ish. Software engineer. Dad to two boys I'm stupid proud of. Carolina Hurricanes addict. Bourbon enthusiast with an increasingly complicated relationship to bourbon. Professional wearer of the same damn hoodie. Recovering believer in the idea that adulthood eventually starts making sense.

It doesn't.

I live in Raleigh. I write code, yell at hockey games, overthink retirement spreadsheets, and have somehow become the guy friends call when life catches on fire. I'm honored by that, even when it's heavy as fuck.

This blog is probably going to wander.

Some days it'll be software, AI, and whatever ridiculous thing Claude or ChatGPT did that morning. Some days it'll be markets, retirement math, or why driving the shitty paid-off car is secretly a superpower. Other days it'll be hockey, because Rod the Bod deserves paragraphs and Tom Wilson deserves fewer nice words than I'm willing to print.

There'll be stories about my boys. Travel. Music. Politics when I can't keep my mouth shut—which, let's be honest, is often. Grief when it shows up. Dumb jokes when they land. Probably too much profanity.

Actually, definitely too much profanity.

Life has a way of reminding you that it's equal parts beautiful and absolutely bananas. People you love leave. Kids grow up. Technology outruns your expectations. Your knees start negotiating with stairs. Somehow you laugh anyway.

That's the plan here.

No brand. No hustle. No productivity gospel. Just one tired engineer trying to make a little sense of the world between hockey games, pull requests, and the occasional existential crisis.

Thanks for stopping by.

Let's fuckin' go.
