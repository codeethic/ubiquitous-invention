Bruvs…

25 is such a weird age. You feel old because college kids look like toddlers, but actual adults still see you as a kid. You’re strong enough to fuck your life up properly for the first time. LoL.

So here’s what I’d tell Jacob and Jackson:

First — nobody knows what the fuck they’re doing.
Seriously. Even successful people are mostly improvising with better confidence and nicer appliances. Don’t confuse certainty with wisdom.

Second — choose your partner carefully.
Holy-ee. This one changes EVERYTHING. Your peace. Your finances. Your confidence. Your home life. Your future kids. Your stress levels. Your ability to breathe easy in your own kitchen.

Pretty face fades into wallpaper if the relationship is chaos. Find somebody kind. Somebody who roots for you. Somebody you can survive boredom, grief and Target runs with.

Third — save money early.
Compound interest is black magic.
Twenty-five year old you thinks retirement is fake. Fifty year old you wants to kiss twenty-five year old you directly on the mouth for maxing the Roth.

Fourth — take care of your body now.
You are borrowing against future cartilage. Every dumb macho decision arrives with interest later. Stretch. Sleep. Lift. Sunscreen. Water. Your back is not invincible, despite current propaganda.

Fifth — call your people.
Friends drift unless somebody does the work. Be the somebody sometimes. Brotherhood takes maintenance.

Sixth — don’t build your identity entirely around work.
Corporate America would replace your corpse before your Slack status turned gray. Grind matters, yes. Pride in work matters. But your humanity has to exist outside productivity metrics.

Seventh — learn emotional honesty earlier than I did.
Suppressing everything feels strong when you’re young. Then one day you’re crying in a Costco parking lot because a Springsteen song hit wrong. Therapy is cheaper than implosion, Bruv.

Eighth — travel.
Go see the world before your knees sound like microwave popcorn and you need six business days to recover from sleeping wrong.

Ninth — be useful.
Learn how to fix shit. Cook. Budget. Change a tire. Calm people down. Explain complicated things simply. Useful humans are gold.

And finally:

Tell people you love them while they can still hear it.

That’s the one.
That’s the real shit.

Because one day the game ends unexpectedly for somebody you thought you had more time with and all the unsent texts in the world won’t save you then.

Love hard.
Laugh often.
Protect your peace.
Don’t vote for fascists.

LFG.
