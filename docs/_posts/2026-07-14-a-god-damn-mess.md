Fuck, Bruv. I think it's a god damn mess.

Iran's regime has spent decades funding proxy groups, threatening its neighbors, repressing its own people, and pushing its nuclear program. I don't have a lot of sympathy for the leadership. Zero tears shed for the assholes running that government.

But...

War has a way of making everyone act like there's a clean solution when there usually isn't. Bombing campaigns don't magically produce stable democracies. Iraq should've beaten that lesson into our heads. You can absolutely set back military capabilities while simultaneously creating ten new problems that'll be around for twenty years.

My bias has always been:

* Keep Iran from getting a nuclear weapon.
* Avoid a full-scale regional war if at all possible.
* Don't get American troops sucked into another forever war because politicians started believing their own press releases.
* Remember it's ordinary people who end up bleeding for the decisions of powerful cunts.

And the thing that worries me most is escalation. One miscalculation, one attack that kills a bunch of Americans or Israelis or Gulf civilians, and suddenly everyone's locked into retaliation because nobody wants to look weak.

That's how shit spirals.

I also think social media has made this worse. Every conflict becomes Team Sports. You're either expected to cheer every bomb or condemn every military action. Reality is usually uglier than that.

So... yeah. Bananas. Dangerous. And I'm skeptical of anyone telling me it's gonna be quick, easy, or consequence-free. History says otherwise.
