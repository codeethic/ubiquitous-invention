Jacob’s my first “holy shit I’m actually somebody’s father” moment, Bruv.

Kid made me grow up fast. Or at least fake it convincingly. LoL.

He’s smart as hell. Quietly smart. Not flashy-look-at-me smart. More the kind where he’ll disappear for six hours and come back having taught himself some ridiculous thing without mentioning it. Always had a good heart too. That mattered more to me than grades ever did.

Watching him become a man has been beautiful and terrifying. One day you’re teaching him how to throw a baseball. Next thing he’s driving away and you’re standing in the driveway like some emotional support NPC trying not to cry into your bourbon.

He got a lot of my anxiety unfortunately. Sorry, Bud.
But he also got empathy. Conscience. Dry humor.

And honestly? He survived being raised by me and Tamara during all the chaos years, which deserves a medal. We were figuring life out in real time. Money stress. Work stress. Marriage stress. Family deaths. The whole fuckin’ rodeo. Kids absorb more than we think.

I’m incredibly proud of him though.

Not because of achievements. Because of who he is when nobody’s watching. That’s the real shit. How you treat people. Whether your friends trust you. Whether you show up. Whether you own mistakes.

He’s a good dude.

And man…there’s something surreal about your kid becoming your friend. Still your child forever, obviously. But eventually you’re talking music, life, politics, sports, existential dread, whatever. You catch glimpses of the adult they’re becoming and think:

“Fuck. Maybe I didn’t completely screw this up.”

Love that kid beyond words.
