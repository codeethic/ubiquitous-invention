Fuck, Bruv. **Poverty.** Big one.

At its core, poverty is not just "not having enough money." It's having so little margin that one bad event — illness, car trouble, missed paycheck, rent increase — can fuck your entire life.

And the thing people with money routinely underestimate is the **poverty tax**.

Folks living on the edge pay more for:

* housing, because they can't afford the deposit or move costs
* transportation, because the cheap car is the one constantly breaking
* food, because bulk buying requires cash and storage
* credit, because desperation makes borrowing expensive
* time, because everything takes longer when you lack resources

Then there's the psychological tax. Constant scarcity eats bandwidth. You're not sitting around thinking about retirement allocation or whether to do a Roth conversion. You're thinking:

**"Can I make it to Friday?"**

And I don't buy the lazy American story that poverty is primarily a character flaw. Some people absolutely make terrible decisions. Rich people make terrible decisions too — they just get called "entrepreneurs" or "investors" instead of idiots.

The big structural problem is that America has decided certain necessities — healthcare, housing, education, childcare — should be treated as markets first and human needs second. That is, frankly, a pretty fucking stupid way to run a civilization.

My take?

**A decent society should guarantee folks living on the edge should not mean being fucked.** Food. Shelter. Healthcare. Education. Basic dignity. Then, sure, argue about incentives and taxes and welfare fraud and all the other stuff people use to avoid looking directly at the actual human beings.

Because if a society can produce billionaires and still have children going hungry, that's not a resource problem.

That's a **political choice**.

And yeah. I find that pretty fucking hard to defend.
