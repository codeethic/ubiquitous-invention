Cleve was one of those dudes who made a room lighter just by walking into it, Bruv.

Funny as fuck. Smart. Could chirp you mercilessly one second then check on your soul the next. The kind of friend you assume will always be there because your brain literally can’t model the world without them in it.

Then one day they’re gone and your entire operating system throws exceptions.

That’s the thing nobody tells you about suicide. It doesn’t just take the person. It rewrites the emotional geometry of everybody left standing. You replay conversations for years. You look for missed signs. You wonder if one extra phone call changes the timeline. It’s brutal.

I carry Cleve with me constantly now. Same with Michael Richardson. Same with Brian. Same with all the people-shaped holes life punches through you as you age. You don’t “get over” that shit. You just learn how to walk while carrying heavier weight.

Part of why I’m obsessive about “watch out for your friends” comes from that.

Be men of action.
Call your people.
Notice changes.
Don’t assume the strong friend is okay.
Especially the funny ones.

And also — talk about your pain. Men are fuckin’ terrible at this historically. We armor up. We isolate. We joke around the wound instead of treating it. That can become deadly.

Cleve mattered. Deeply.

Love ya, Bruv.
